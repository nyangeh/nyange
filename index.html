HCI CHAPTER ONE(Introduction)

In the ﬁrst edition of this book we wrote the following: This is the authors’ second attempt at writing this introduction. Our ﬁrst attempt fell victim to a design quirk coupled with an innocent, though weary and less than attentive, user. The word-processing package we originally used to write this introduction is menu based. Menu items are grouped to reﬂect their function. The ‘save’ and ‘delete’ options, both of which are correctly classiﬁed as ﬁle-level operations, are consequently adjacent items in the menu. With a cursor controlled by a trackball it is all too easy for the hand to slip, inadvertently selecting delete instead of save. Of course, the delete option, being well thought out, pops up a conﬁrmation box allowing the user to cancel a mistaken command. Unfortunately, the save option produces a very similar conﬁrmation box – it was only as we hit the ‘Conﬁrm’ button that we noticed the word ‘delete’ at the top...

research methodology

This study conducted through qualitative research methodology. This gives respondent to give their understanding of reason for first year students decide to withdraw from their university programme. The approach gives freedom to respondent and it was easy to conduct. This study uses qualitative methods which are; • Structured interview with Mzumbe university students who have already withdraw or exit their programs during their first year. • Focus group such as Mzumbe staffs, faculty of science and technology (FST) students. Structured interview and focus group discussion were used for assessing former student, administrative staffs of Mzumbe and faculty of science and technology. Structured interview and focus group analyzed important issues like; • What are the impact to the academic performance for student’s withdrawal their courses? • Does the number of student’s withdrawal affect the administrative working of particular organization? • What are the solutions taken by the government to ensure proper course selection to students?
